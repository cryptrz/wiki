# Luxembourg-Findel Airport (ELLX)

---

### **Summary**

Luxembourg-Findel Airport (ELLX) uses standard VHF frequencies for ATC communication. The main frequencies for Approach, Tower, and ATIS are as follows. ADS-B typically operates on 1090 MHz for Mode S transponders, which is standard worldwide and applies to Luxembourg as well.

---

### **Details**

Luxembourg-Findel Airport (ELLX) Frequencies

| Frequency (MHz) | Service | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 120.885 | Luxembourg Approach | Primary approach control |
| 118.105 | Luxembourg Tower | Main tower frequency |
| 134.755 | ATIS | Automatic Terminal Information Service |

---

### **Explanation**

- **ADS-B Frequency**: ADS-B (Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast) in Europe and worldwide operates on **1090 MHz** for Mode S transponders (used by most commercial and general aviation aircraft). This is a global standard and applies to Luxembourg-Findel Airport as well. ADS-B ground stations, including those at major airports, receive these signals to provide real-time aircraft tracking and surveillance.
- **Approach (APP)**: 120.885 MHz is used for communication with aircraft on approach to or departing from Luxembourg-Findel Airport.
- **Tower (TWR)**: 118.105 MHz is the main frequency for ground control and takeoff/landing clearances.
- **ATIS**: 134.755 MHz provides automated weather, runway, and airport information to pilots.
